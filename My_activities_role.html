Application on Stop Killing Mass in Mayanmar

"Stop the Rohingya Genocide, Humanity Knows No Borders"

Date: September 20, 2017
Organized by: Youth Ending Hunger Bangladesh

Background:
The Rohingya Genocide in Myanmar
The Rohingya crisis is one of the most tragic humanitarian issues of recent times. The Rohingya, a Muslim ethnic minority in Myanmar Rakhine State, have faced systematic oppression, violence, and displacement for decades. The situation escalated in August 2017 when the Myanmar military launched a brutal crackdown on the Rohingya population, leading to mass killings, forced displacement, and destruction of villages. Reports from human rights organizations highlighted widespread atrocities, including murder, women oppression, and arson. As a result, nearly one million Rohingya refugees were forced to flee to neighboring Bangladesh, seeking safety and humanitarian aid. Human Chain Protest: Our Engagement
To protest against this inhumane genocide and demand justice for the Rohingya community, Youth Ending Hunger Bangladesh organized a human chain demonstration on September 20, 2017. The protest aimed to raise global awareness, call for action against Myanmar government, and push for stronger international intervention to stop the killings. Key Activities of the Protest Formation of the Human Chain: Hundreds of participants, including students, activists, and concerned citizens, joined hands to form a long human chain, symbolizing unity and solidarity with the Rohingya people. Strong Speech & Protest Slogans: Protesters delivered powerful speeches condemning the genocide, urging world leaders and the United Nations to take immediate action. Slogans such as:
"Stop Rohingya Genocide"
"Justice for Rohingya Victims"
"End Ethnic Cleansing in Myanmar"
"Human Rights for Rohingya No More Violence"
Throughout the demonstration, amplifying the message.

Public Signature Campaign: A public sign campaign was organized where protesters and passersby signed petitions demanding accountability and justice. The petition was later submitted to relevant authorities, including human rights organizations.
Media & Awareness: The demonstration received significant attention from media outlets, spreading the message beyond Bangladesh and encouraging international discussions on the Rohingya crisis.
Impact & Conclusion: The human chain protest was a strong statement of solidarity with the oppressed Rohingya community. Through our participation, we demanded global accountability, protection of human rights, and urgent humanitarian aid. The movement reinforced the responsibility of youth and civil society in speaking out against injustice and advocating for peace and human dignity.

My Role in the Human Chain Protest
During the human chain protest organized by Youth Ending Hunger Bangladesh on September 20, 2017, I actively participated in raising awareness and demonstrating my support for the Rohingya crisis. I expressed my deep condolence for the innocent lives lost and the suffering faced by the Rohingya community due to the mass killings and forced displacement in Myanmar.

To contribute meaningfully to the movement:
I signed the petition, adding my voice to the demand for justice and international intervention. My participation in this initiative reinforced the collective effort to push for action against the genocide.
I drew posters carrying strong messages and visuals that depicted the horrors of the Rohingya crisis. These posters played a crucial role in grabbing public attention and spreading awareness about the cause.
I stood in solidarity with fellow protesters in the human chain, chanting slogans and advocating for peace, human rights, and an end to ethnic cleansing.
Through my engagement in this protest, I made a meaningful contribution to amplifying the voices of the oppressed and strengthening the movement for justice. It was a moment of responsibility and activism, reaffirming the power of youth in standing against violence and human rights violations.
