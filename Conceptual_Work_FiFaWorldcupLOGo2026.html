The structure was inspired by a baby girl sitting on the floor and playing. The star represents the host cities of the tournament, while the maple leaf symbolizes Canadian nationality. The trophy is incorporated as a central element.

The head of the structure divides into three parts to showcase the vintage ball used in the first World Cup tournament.

The body design combines three elements representing the landscapes of the three beautiful host countries. It features a symbolic lake and forms the foundation for the trophy's elegant model.

This element captures the Atlantic Ocean waves meeting the North American coastline, symbolizing the connection between continents through football.

A celebration of the joint World Cup hosted by three North American countries, honoring football history while capturing special moments from past tournaments.

Inspired by Jarabe Tapatío, the Mexican national dance, this element represents the perfect expression of joy and celebration that football brings to the world.

Featuring the Questra pattern from the 1994 World Cup ball, this pays homage to the first World Cup organized by the United States of America.

Representing the six football confederations united in tournament spirit, this element shows their connection through the beautiful game, leading to spectacular final moments.

This element captures the global excitement for the world's greatest tournament, while also nodding to NASA's upcoming moon mission, symbolizing humanity's boundless aspirations.

This is a conceptual design proposal for the upcoming FIFA World Cup 2026 tournament. Created between November 2022 and May 2023, this artwork represents a special journey. The primary vision is to remind FIFA that this event belongs not just to a select few, but to the entire world - uniting nations through the beautiful game.
